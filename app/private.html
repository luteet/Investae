What is Private debt?

One of the main consequences of the 2008/2011 financial crisis is the reinforcement of the regulatory capital requirement for banks on the European market (Basel, 3 and 4).


The stricter regulatory environment has made it very expensive for banks to hold some types of loans on their balance sheets. As a results, many SMEs have left aside without any possibility to find financing. They are turning to private debts instruments. Private debt includes all forms of private lending whether with ISIN or without ISIN.

Private debt is a new asset class.
It is attractive because:

  • 01 Private offers fixed income to investors.
  • 03 Private debt offers higher yield in exchange for a lower liquidity.
  • 02 Private debt is used mainly for acquisitions, refinancing or growth capital.
  • 04 Private debt generally comes with protection : collaterals, guarantees, insurance.
